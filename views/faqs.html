Frequently Asked Questions

1. How do I pay for my ride on the Duquesne Incline? +

Please have exact cash fare upon arrival. The Duquesne Incline operates using a CASH fare system, similar to Pittsburghâ€™s buses and trolleys. No tickets, no advance payments, and no debit or credit cards are accepted. A change machine is available for your convenience.
-
2. Do I need to purchase a ticket in advance? +

No, tickets are not required in advance. Simply pay the exact fare when you arrive. There are no reservations required to ride the Duquesne Incline.
-
3. Are there any discounts available for seniors? +

Yes! Pennsylvania Seniors 65 and older who present a BLUE or YELLOW PRT card or a Senior ConnectCard can ride for free. Otherwise, the standard adult fare applies. Please note, the Duquesne Incline is required to ask for a Pennsylvania Senior Citizen Transit ID card as proof of discount eligibility for reimbursement by the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania. No exceptions.
-
4. Are restrooms available? +

Unfortunately, there are no restrooms available at the Duquesne Incline stations. We encourage visitors to plan accordingly before their visit.
-
5. What are the busiest times to visit the Duquesne Incline? +

Saturdays and Sundays after noon tend to be the busiest times. Expect to wait in line for approximately 10 minutes in both directions during these peak hours.
-
6. Is the Duquesne Incline accessible for individuals with disabilities? +

Yes! The Upper Station, located at 1220 Grandview Ave, Pittsburgh, PA 15211, is wheelchair accessible. Our observation deck is also wheelchair accessible, providing scenic views of Pittsburgh. The Historic Incline cars can accommodate wheelchairs that are up to 25 inches in width.
